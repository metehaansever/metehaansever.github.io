Research

I am a biogeochemist with particular interests in soil microbial processes and proteomics methods development.

I study the microbiology of Arctic soils using multi-omics data. This work has produced insights into the niche differentiation of major bacterial groups and the biogeochemical consequences of floral changes caused by global warming. I also develop bioinformatic tools for the analysis of protein mass spectrometry data. I have improved the de novo sequencing of peptide mass spectra with my postprocessing program, Postnovo, enabling the characterization of proteins from any source without a predefined reference database. Another program, ProteinExpress, analyzes complex proteomes with meta-genomic/transcriptomic reference data, greatly increasing the coverage and human interpretability of protein functions and taxonomic affiliations.

Teaching

I have developed new college environmental chemistry labs which I am preparing for publication in the Journal of Chemical Education. One involves measuring mercury levels in fish by atomic absorption spectroscopy and modeling blood mercury from the data. The other involves benchtop microcosms of the bioremediation of a persistant organic pollutant and the application of the rate measurements to a model of industrial wastewater treatment under a cost constraint.
